NOTE: Guidelines for the summer practice report of METU IE third year students doing SP at manufacturing firms.

---

REPORT FORMAT AND STYLE:
- Same as IE 300: English, numbered headings in capitals, subtitles in lowercase underlined, page numbers, numbered figures/tables.

Report sections:
1. Table of contents (with page numbers)
2. Introduction (scope of SP; main difficulties)
3. Main body (detailed work including IE problem or contributed project sections)
4. Conclusion (evaluation of experience; feedback)
5. References
6. Appendix
7. Glossary (advisable, including company-specific jargon)

- Reports due within first two weeks of following academic term.
- Questions may be reorganized as long as all are answered.
- Unanswerable questions may be skipped with clear justification.

---

EVALUATION OF THE REPORTS:
Two separate scores — students must pass BOTH:

QUESTIONS SECTION (200 points):
- Introductory Features: 10 points
- Analysis of Macro Aspects: 20 points
- An Overview of the Production System: 35 points
- Production Planning and Control System: 50 points
- Quality Planning and Control System: 20 points
- Management Information System: 20 points
- Work Study: 20 points
- Conclusion: 15 points
- Style and Organization: 10 points

Questions grading rules:
- Must score at least 50% in EACH section.
- Total 125-160 points OR any section < 50% → "Incomplete" — returned for revision.
- Total < 125 → "Unsatisfactory" — SP must be repeated at different workplace.

PROBLEM / PROJECT SECTION (100 points):
Evaluated on: problem/project context (IE relevance), technical content, style and organization.
- Score 40-65 → "Incomplete" — returned for revision.
- Score < 40 → "Unsatisfactory" — SP must be repeated.

PROJECT-TYPE SP REPORT (100 points breakdown):
- Introduction (company description): 5 points
- Literature Review: 10 points
- Problem Definition: 30 points
- Data Gathering and Analysis: 10 points
- Solution Approaches: 20 points
- Results: 15 points
- Conclusion: 10 points

Must also fill out online questionnaire by submission date — otherwise report is incomplete.

SECTION 1: INTRODUCTORY FEATURES (10 points)
Q1.1: Full title, founding date, location (full mailing address).
Q1.2: Type of ownership, main shareholders and shares. Partnership, joint venture, franchise, holding, multinational?
Q1.3: Sector and typical products/services. Include advertising brochures/catalogues in appendix. Domestic and international market shares.
Q1.4: Customers (end users, retailers, manufacturers, employees). Identify stakeholders.
Q1.5: Functions performed by industrial engineers.

---

SECTION 2: ANALYSIS OF MACRO ASPECTS (20 points)
Q2.1: Sketch the production system as a black box — inputs and outputs. Does the firm export? Import raw materials? Standards and certificates (ISO, TSE, EN, EC)?
Q2.2: State the mission and vision of the practice organization. Differences in missions for different products/services?
Q2.3: Most prevailing factor for facility location (distribution needs, raw materials, labor, transportation, suppliers, environment, laws/taxation, land cost). Support with quantitative/qualitative observations.

CHOOSE ONE of 2.4 or 2.5:
Q2.4: Capital investment plans and proposals. Evaluation methods and criteria. Effects of inflation. Example of past investment evaluation.
Q2.5: How did firm obtain know-how/technologies? Improvements/upgrades? Licensed trademarks/patents? R&D unit? Project management for capital investments or technology adoptions?

---

SECTION 3: AN OVERVIEW OF THE PRODUCTION SYSTEM (35 points)
Q3.1: Process (operations) chart of a major product/subassembly, routing for a group of products, or service process.
Q3.2: How is rated capacity defined and measured? (Rated capacity = Designed capacity × Utilization × Efficiency)
Q3.3: Type of operations — continuous, repetitive/flow shop, intermittent (job shop/batch shop/project shop). Also: MTS, MTO, or ATO classification.
Q3.4: Draw a block plan of the practice organization (or provide blueprint).
Q3.5: Discuss layout types (process, product, cellular/GT, fixed-position, or combination).
Q3.6: Select a department — indicate layout type and discuss how characteristics fit.
Q3.7: Unit-manufacturing costs — how calculated? (direct material + direct labor + overhead). Provide example.
Q3.8: Comparative ratio analysis using balance sheets and income statements for last two years, OR itemize balance sheet/income statement titles.

---

SECTION 4: PRODUCTION PLANNING AND CONTROL SYSTEM (50 points)
(Answer at least 3 questions)

Q4.1: Forecasting activities — purposes, methods, data sources, model used, accuracy measurement, computer support. Provide example.
Q4.2: Who decides what and how much to produce? How? What resources are scarce? Current limits? Main concerns in allocation (customer satisfaction, cost, time, utilization)? How are plans made and reviewed?
Q4.3: Major inventory items by function. Reasons for holding (uncertainty, seasonal, cycle stocks, quantity discounts, pipeline). Inventory control policies (periodic vs continuous review). MRP activity? Performance measures.
Q4.4: Detailed scheduling activities. Records (timetables, daily lists, charts)? Example. Apply performance measure on schedule implementation.
Q4.5: JIT philosophy — organized studies to reduce slack? Example of allowance/slack used to assure uninterrupted operation.

SECTION 5: QUALITY PLANNING AND CONTROL SYSTEM (20 points)
(Answer at least 2 questions)

Q5.1: Choose a product — how does the organization define quality? How are customer requirements translated into specifications? Quality control activities throughout product life cycle (design, process design, delivery, usage).
Q5.2: At least three proactive (preventive) quality assurance activities. How is responsibility of quality shared (quality organization)?

SECTION 6: MANAGEMENT INFORMATION SYSTEM (20 points)
(Answer at least 1 question)

Q6.1: Decision makers and subjects at strategic, tactical, and operational levels. One activity per level.
Q6.2: Computer systems — types, capacities, spread across departments. Types of data recorded/processed in a specific division. Level of decision supported.
Q6.3: Software used — ERP, reservation system, stock keeping, accounting (not standard office programs). Level of decision supported. Example.

SECTION 7: WORK STUDY (20 points)
(Answer at least 1 question)

Q7.1: Apply a work measurement technique (stopwatch, predetermined time standards, work sampling) to an operator. Evaluate results. Apply method study for more effective methods.
Q7.2: Has there been a Job Evaluation study? If yes, describe method and implementation. If no, how is wage differentiation determined?

APPENDIX A: DEFINITION AND FORMULATION OF IE PROBLEMS

Step 1 — Identification and Definition:
- Observe symptoms (deficiencies in planning, low utilization, inventory problems).
- Validate symptoms with objective data.
- Use analysis tools: cause-effect diagrams, Pareto analysis, relations diagram, logic tree, flowcharts, check sheets, rich pictures, brainstorming, run charts, influence diagrams, multivoting, nominal group technique, stratification, affinity diagrams, precedence diagrams.

Step 2 — Formulation:
- Decision maker (owner of the problem)
- Goal/objective (direction and satisfactory amount of improvement)
- Performance criteria
- Alternative courses of action
- Limitations, restrictions, requirements
- Analysis of interactions with subsystems
- Comment on specific modeling approaches.

APPENDIX B: REPORTING ON A PARTICIPATED PROJECT

If project is COMPLETED, report must include:
1. Introduction (products/processes, context, team composition)
2. Problem statement (issue addressed, IE relevance)
3. Approach taken (planned stages, modifications, your role)
4. Summary of work done (data gathered, methods, results)
5. Conclusions and recommendations (outcomes, suggestions, extensions)

If project is STILL IN PROGRESS, report must include:
1. Introduction
2. Problem statement
3. Approach taken (planned stages, your role)
4. Project schedule (activity network or Gantt chart)
5. Work done to date (data gathered so far, methods, results to date)
6. Expectations of outcomes (prospective benefits, evaluation)
