PAGE: Custom Frequently Asked Questions (Student-Oriented)
SOURCE: Compiled from official sp-ie.metu.edu.tr content and common student concerns
---

Q: What is the difference between IE 300 and IE 400?
A: IE 300 is the first summer practice course, typically done after the 2nd or 3rd year. IE 400 is the second summer practice course, done in a later year. IE 400 requires a more in-depth report including a problem definition/project component. IE 400 also allows project-based internships of at least 6 weeks with prior proposal approval.

Q: How many workdays do I need for summer practice?
A: A standard summer practice is at least 20 workdays (4 weeks). For project-based IE 400, the minimum is 30 workdays (6 weeks).

Q: Can I do my summer practice abroad?
A: Yes. You can do your summer practice abroad. If you go through the Erasmus Student Internship mobility program, you can do an internship in Europe for 3-12 months. A short-term Erasmus internship (3 months) can only count as one of IE300 or IE400, not both. You will still need SGK insurance arrangements through the university.

Q: Where do I submit my summer practice report?
A: SP reports are collected via ODTUClass. The deadline will be announced on ODTUClass at the beginning of the semester. Reports must be in PDF format.

Q: Can my report be generated by AI tools?
A: No. Reports will be checked for plagiarism and whether they are generated by AI tools before evaluation.

Q: Who evaluates my report?
A: Faculty members will be assigned to evaluate your report. If corrections are needed, they will be returned in the last month of the semester. You have 2-3 weeks to complete corrections.

Q: How do I contact the SP Committee?
A: Email: ie-staj@metu.edu.tr. You can also visit committee members in their offices (see SP Committee page for room numbers and phone numbers). For document submissions related to SP evaluation forms, email sp-belge@metu.edu.tr.

Q: Do I need to physically attend the workplace?
A: Yes. Summer internships must be done by physically attending the workplace. Remote internships are not accepted.

Q: What if I get paid during my internship?
A: If you did your SP in a private company in Turkey and received payment, you need to: (1) fill in the "Paid SP form questionnaire" on OCW, (2) upload the signed Paid Summer Practice Form, and (3) upload the bank receipt showing payment. This does not apply to public institutions.

Q: Can I do a voluntary (extra) internship?
A: Yes. The university provides SGK insurance for voluntary internships up to one month for one company. The voluntary internship is between the student and the company, and no letter from the committee is needed. Note: Foreign students are NOT allowed to do voluntary internships in Turkey.

Q: When should I apply for SGK insurance?
A: Apply 2-3 weeks before your internship starts via the OCW system. Do not apply too early (not 2 months before) or too late (not 5 days before). Leave a safety margin of 1 full week between your application and the start date.

Q: Where can I find my SGK document?
A: Your SGK document will be available on e-devlet (turkiye.gov.tr) typically 2-3 workdays before your SP begins. Search for "işe giriş" and select "4A İşe Giriş Çıkış Bildirgesi".

Q: What documents does the company need to fill out?
A: The company (your SP supervisor) needs to fill out and sign: (1) SP Evaluation Form, and (2) Employer Survey. These can be sent to sp-belge@metu.edu.tr in PDF format, signed traditionally or digitally. They can also be sent by post in a closed envelope.

Q: What if my internship gets cancelled after I applied for SGK?
A: Contact the SP Committee at ie-staj@metu.edu.tr immediately.

Q: Can two students submit the same report if they intern at the same company?
A: No. Each student must have a separate report. For IE 400, the general outlook of the firm can be common. The questions part must be different. Problem definitions must be different. The project part may be the same only if the project is complex (valid for at most two students).

Q: I need my SP contract (staj sözleşmesi) signed. What do I do?
A: Leave the contract at the Undergraduate Secretary Office (IE 129). You can pick up the signed form in one week.

Q: How do I apply for internships at public institutions in Turkey?
A: All public institutions accept internship applications through the centralized Kariyer Kapısı system at https://kariyerkapisi.cbiko.gov.tr/
