PREREQUISITES FOR IE 400:
- IE 300 +
- IE 252, IE 323, IE 333 +
- Any two courses from the set: {IE 304, IE 324, IE 372, IE 368}
- If any one of the prerequisite courses is not completed with a grade >= DD, IE400 cannot be conducted.

---

IE 400 - WHEN TO CONDUCT:
- Conduct IE 400 summer practice in Summer 2025.
- Available time interval (~3 months): June 25 – September 26.
- If you attend summer school, your summer practice period should not overlap with summer school.
- Minimum practice is 20 workdays (4 weeks). This duration can be more if the company requests it.
- You can conduct IE 400 during semester break in winter as well, if 20 workdays are available in the break.
- If you are in graduation status and have completed all course work, you can conduct both IE 300 and IE 400 in succession in Summer 2025.

---

REGISTRATION FOR IE 400:
- After SP is completed, you must register for IE 400 in the immediate following academic term (Fall 2025-2026).
- Summer practice reports are due within the first 4 weeks of the registered academic term.
- Due date: October 24, 2025.

STEP 1: SEARCH FOR ALTERNATIVE COMPANIES

Accepted company types for IE 400:

Manufacturing firms (same as IE 300):
- Automotive, Machine parts, Electronics, Furniture, Textiles, Consumer durables
- Batch process industries: Steel, Paper mills, Pharmaceutical, Food and beverages

ALSO accepted for IE 400 (unlike IE 300):
- Continuous process industries: Cement, concrete, Sugar, Flour mill
- Service industries: Hospitals, Hotels, Banking, Research organizations (ODTÜ Teknokent, etc.), Transportation, cargo carriers, Public institutions, Non-governmental organizations (NGOs)

KEY DIFFERENCE FROM IE 300: IE 400 accepts both manufacturing AND service companies, while IE 300 only accepts manufacturing companies.

The department lists firms on the SP website > SP Opportunities.

Work mode rules:
- Manufacturing company: summer practice must be totally face-to-face (in the plant).
- Service company: summer practice can be either face-to-face or hybrid (face-to-face + online).

---

STEP 2: EVALUATE THE COMPANY
- Check the IE 400 Manual from https://sp-ie.metu.edu.tr > Documents/Forms tab.
- Two types of manuals: (i) manufacturing manual and (ii) service manual.
- Check whether you can answer the questions without much difficulty.
- Consider: Number of employees? How many engineers, IEs? Production capacity? Information system? ERP?
- The SP Committee does not examine and confirm the suitability of your SP company in advance.
- Students determine whether the company is appropriate.
- Discuss with SP Committee if unsure.

---

STEP 3: APPLY TO THE COMPANY
- Same as IE 300. Use SP Application Form and/or SP Protocol from Documents/Forms tab.
- You can apply to more than one company; for each company, fill out a separate form.
- After filling the protocol form, leave it at Undergraduate Secretary Office, IE 128. Pick up in a week.

---

STEP 4: APPLY FOR THE INSURANCE
- Same procedure as IE 300 via METU OCW.
- Apply 2 weeks before the beginning of your SP.
- Upload Declaration Form to OCW.
- Get "Sigortalı İşe Başlama Belgesi" from e-devlet when ready.
- Compulsory insurance: iş kazası ve meslek hastalığı sigortası (Law #5510).
- For compulsory SPs: insurance for 20 workdays to 3 months.
- For voluntary SPs: insurance for one month at most for one organization.
- Insurance is made for summer practices abroad as well.
- If company insures you, no need to apply to the university.

---

STEP 5: CONDUCT YOUR SUMMER PRACTICE

Three modes of IE 400 SP:

1. Individual:
   - At least 20 workdays
   - Write your own report

2. Group of two students:
   - At least 20 workdays
   - Each writes his/her own report
   - Project/problem should be much more involved
   - Notify the SP committee in the first week of practice

3. Project-based:
   - At least 6 weeks (30 workdays)
   - Two-page proposal must be approved by SP Committee before SP starts
   - Content and format is different (see the manual)

Other tasks during SP:
- Download "Evaluation Form and Employer Survey Form" from Documents/Forms tab.
- Attach a recent photograph to the first page.
- Company fills out forms at the end of SP and emails to sp-belge@metu.edu.tr.
- Search for a "Systems Design Project" during your SP — it is a good opportunity to find SD projects.
- Look for interesting problems; ask engineers about potential challenging problems for the SD project.
- If you identify a problem, check with the SD committee (NOT the SP committee).

---

STEP 6: SUBMIT YOUR REPORT
- Upload SP report and questionnaire at odtuclass by the announced due date.
- The report consists of two sections: "Questions" and "Problem/Project" — graded separately. Grade S is required in both parts to pass IE 400.
- IE 400 manuals are of two types: (i) manufacturing manual and (ii) service manual.
- In some questions (e.g. accounting, finance), provide information about the method used at least.
- For each question, provide sufficient explanation and analysis; figure/table only is not accepted.
- Read the explanation in each question and answer accordingly.
- You can start writing your report during the practice.
- Use "Google Grammarly" to correct the report before submission.
- Submit soft copy (pdf: docx converted to pdf) at odtuclass IE 400 – turnitin assignment. Similarity must be < 20%.
- Reports are also checked whether they are AI generated.
- For the questionnaire: use METU Survey (info from SP Committee at beginning of semester).
- If you received payment: fill "Paid SP questionnaire" at OCW, upload signed Paid SP Form and bank receipt as a Zip file.

VOLUNTARY SP:
- Possible to do a voluntary internship for additional work experience.
- University provides insurance after completing second year.
- For voluntary SPs: insurance for one month at most for one organization.
- Follow the same insurance application procedure.
